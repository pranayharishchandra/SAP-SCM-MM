# üåü **What is P2P Cycle (Procure-to-Pay Cycle)?**

**P2P = Procure to Pay**
It means the **entire process from buying materials (procure)** to **paying the vendor (pay)**.

üëâ It starts when a **department needs some material** and ends when the **vendor receives the payment**.

## üßæ **1Ô∏è‚É£ Purchase Requisition (PR)**

**Meaning:**
A **Purchase Requisition (PR)** is an **internal document** that tells the Purchase Department ‚Äî

> ‚ÄúWe need this material in this quantity at this time.‚Äù

### **Who creates it?**

Any **department** can create it:

- **Production Dept:** needs raw materials
- **Maintenance Dept:** needs spare parts
- **HR/Admin:** needs office items

### **Example:**

| Field         | Example         |
| ------------- | --------------- |
| Material      | X               |
| Quantity      | 100             |
| Delivery Date | 15th Nov        |
| Plant         | Mumbai          |
| Requested By  | Production Dept |

### **SAP Transaction:** `ME51N` ‚Äì Create PR

üëâ **Key idea:**
**PR is just a request ‚Äî not yet an order.**
It needs to be **checked and approved** before sending to vendors.

## üè∑Ô∏è **2Ô∏è‚É£ Vendor Selection (RFQ & Quotation Comparison)**

After PR is approved, the **Purchase Department** starts **finding vendors** who can supply the material.

---

### **A. Request for Quotation (RFQ)**

- Purchase Dept sends a **Request for Quotation (RFQ)** to several vendors.
- It includes details like:

  - Material name
  - Quantity
  - Delivery date
  - Delivery location

**Example:**

> "Dear Vendor, please quote your best price for Material X, Quantity 100, to be delivered in 7 days."

---

### **B. Vendors Send Quotations**

Each vendor replies with:

| Vendor   | Price (‚Çπ/piece) | Delivery Time |
| -------- | --------------- | ------------- |
| Vendor A | 10              | 5 days        |
| Vendor B | 8               | 4 days        |
| Vendor C | 18              | 3 days        |

---

### **C. Quotation Comparison**

Purchase team compares all quotations by **price, quality, and delivery time**.

In SAP, this is done using a **Price Comparison List**.

**Example Decision:**

> Vendor B gives ‚Çπ8 per piece ‚Äî best price and good delivery.
> ‚úÖ **Select Vendor B.**

### **D. Create Purchase Order (PO) for selected vendor**

After selecting Vendor B ‚Üí

## üìú **3Ô∏è‚É£ Purchase Order (PO)**

**Meaning:**
A **Purchase Order** is the **official order** sent to the vendor.

### **Details inside a PO:**

- Vendor name
- Material & quantity

- Delivery date & address

### **Example:**

| Field         | Example      |
| ------------- | ------------ |

| Material      | X            |
| Quantity      | 100          |

üëâ **Key idea:**
**PO = legal agreement** between company and vendor.

## üì¶ **4Ô∏è‚É£ Goods Receipt (GR)**

**Meaning:**
When the vendor delivers the material, the **warehouse/store team** checks it and **posts Goods Receipt (GR)** in SAP.

### **Purpose of GR:**

- To confirm that **goods have arrived**.
- To **increase stock** in the system.
- To create a **Material Document** and **Accounting Document**.

### **Example:**

| Material | Quantity | Movement Type | Stock Type         |
| -------- | -------- | ------------- | ------------------ |
| X        | 100      | 101           | Unrestricted Stock |

### **SAP Transaction:** `MIGO` ‚Äì Goods Movement (used for GR)

After GR:

- The system updates **Inventory Stock**.
- It also updates **PO History** (shows GR posted).

üëâ **Key idea:**
**GR = physical entry of goods into your store.**

## üí∞ **5Ô∏è‚É£ Invoice Receipt (IR)**

Now comes the **Invoice stage**, where the **vendor sends the bill** for the delivered goods.

---

### ‚ùì **Is "Invoice" and "Invoice Receipt" different?**

Yes! Let‚Äôs understand clearly üëá

| **Term**                 | **Meaning**                                                                                                           |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Invoice**              | The **bill** sent by the **vendor** (external document).                                                              |
| **Invoice Receipt (IR)** | The **record created by the company** in SAP when they **receive and post** the vendor‚Äôs invoice (internal document). |

So:

- Vendor sends Invoice (PDF or paper).
- Company enters that data in SAP ‚Üí that becomes **Invoice Receipt (IR)**.

---

### **Purpose of Invoice Receipt (IR):**

- To **record vendor‚Äôs bill** in the system.
- To **match** the Invoice with **PO** and **GR** ‚Äî called **3-Way Match**.

**3-Way Match = PO + GR + Invoice**
‚úÖ All three should have:

- Same material
- Same quantity
- Same price

If all match ‚Üí system allows **posting of invoice**.

If not ‚Üí system blocks it for review (price difference, missing GR, etc.)

### **SAP Transaction:** `MIRO` ‚Äì Enter Invoice

After invoice posting:

- Vendor‚Äôs **liability** is created in the **Accounts Payable** system.
- Example: Vendor B ‚Üí Company owes ‚Çπ800.

üëâ **Key idea:**
**IR = system record of vendor‚Äôs invoice that triggers payment process.**

---

## üí∏ **6Ô∏è‚É£ Payment to Vendor**

Finally, the **Finance Department** makes the payment to the vendor.

### **When payment happens:**

- As per **payment terms** mentioned in PO (e.g., 30 days after invoice).

### **SAP Process:**

- Payment run (automatic or manual) through **F-53 / F110** transaction.
- System clears the open invoice and updates Accounts Payable.

### **Example:**

> Vendor B ‚Üí Invoice ‚Çπ800 ‚Üí Paid on 15 Dec ‚Üí Status: ‚ÄúCleared.‚Äù

üëâ **Key idea:**
**Payment closes the P2P cycle.**
